Museum Staff

The researchers and interpreters behind the collections

Dr. Leila Navarro — Archaeologist

Expertise: Archaeology, artifact recovery, digital stratigraphy

Dr. Leila Navarro is an archaeologist who studies artifacts to understand everyday life in past cultures. She earned an M.A. in Archaeology and specializes in careful excavation, mapping, and cataloging. Her work uses drones and 3D documentation to preserve fragile artifacts. Her style is calm, methodical, and detail-oriented.

At the museum, she leads field recovery standards and trains interns in documentation. She has received a research grant for rapid 3D scanning methods.

Dr. Marcus Okoye — Physical Anthropologist

Expertise: Human remains, burial context, ethical analysis

Dr. Marcus Okoye studies human remains to learn about health and living conditions in the past. He earned a Ph.D. with a focus on ethical research and respectful handling practices. Marcus uses measurement tools and 3D scans to protect fragile materials.

At the museum, he oversees ethical protocols and advises on responsible exhibits. He supports consultation with descendant communities and repatriation.

Avery Sato — Museum Interpreter

Discipline: Cultural history and symbolic interpretation

Avery Sato is a museum interpreter who turns research into clear cultural stories. They use symbolic analysis and decolonial frameworks to present multiple perspectives. Avery works closely with researchers to connect data with meaning.

At the museum, Avery designs exhibit narratives that encourage critical thinking, transparency, and inclusive storytelling.
