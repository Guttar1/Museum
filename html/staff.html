Museum Staff

The researchers and interpreters behind the collections

Dr. Leila Navarro — Archaeologist

Expertise: Archaeology, artifact recovery, digital stratigraphy

Dr. Leila Navarro is an archaeologist who studies artifacts such as tools, pottery, and structural remains to understand everyday life in past cultures. She earned an M.A. in Archaeology and was trained in meticulous field documentation methods. Leila specializes in mapping and cataloging finds using drones, photogrammetry, and GIS tools. Her working style is calm, methodical, and highly detail-oriented.

At the museum, she leads field recovery standards and trains interns in artifact documentation and preservation. Leila has received a small research grant for developing rapid 3D documentation methods for fragile artifacts.

Dr. Marcus Okoye — Physical Anthropologist

Expertise: Human remains, burial context, ethical analysis

Dr. Marcus Okoye is a physical anthropologist who studies human remains to learn about health, diet, and living conditions in the past. He earned a Ph.D. with a strong focus on ethical research and respectful handling practices. Marcus uses careful measurement, photography, and 3D scanning to protect fragile materials.

At the museum, he oversees ethical protocols for human remains and advises on responsible exhibit design. Marcus actively supports consultation with descendant communities and repatriation when appropriate.

Avery Sato — Museum Interpreter

Discipline: Cultural history, symbolic analysis, interpretation

Avery Sato is a museum interpreter who specializes in turning evidence into clear, meaningful stories for visitors. They studied cultural history and communication and often use symbolic and decolonial frameworks when building narratives. Avery collaborates closely with researchers to connect data with lived experience.

At the museum, Avery designs exhibit text and interpretive materials that present multiple perspectives. They advocate for transparency, cultural sensitivity, and inclusive storytelling.
